<h3 id = "beginning3">Manual Check&nbsp;</h3><h4 style="padding-left: 30px;">Visual &nbsp;Test</h4><ol><ol><li>Visually inspect the page for screen movement.</li><li>&nbsp;Visually check if your moving content has any pause or stop controls</li><li>Evaluate the movement rate visually. As a reference, moving content should not flash more than three seconds during a one-second period.&nbsp;</li></ol></ol><h4 style="padding-left: 30px;">Screen Movement Equation</h4><p style="padding-left: 30px;">The <a href="https://www.section508.va.gov/support/sw/k_1.asp">Department of Veterans Affairs</a> has a recommended testing method for screen movement. &nbsp;</p><ol><ol><li>Count how many times the element flashes within 10 seconds.</li><li>Divide the amount of times by 10 and that is how many times the element flashes per second.</li><li>Results: For the flashing element to comply with Section 508 standards it must flash less than three times in any one second period.</li></ol></ol><h3>Automated Testing Tools</h3><h4 style="padding-left: 30px;"><a title="http://wave.webaim.org/" href="http://wave.webaim.org/" target="_self">WebAIM WAVE tool</a></h4><p style="padding-left: 30px;">Activate the WAVE toolbar and navigate to the “Details” panel. In that panel navigate to the “Features” section and look for any errors related to screen movement. The following accessibility errors can be identified by WAVE:&nbsp;</p><ul><ul><li>&nbsp;the page has a blinking element&nbsp;</li><li>&nbsp;the page has a Marquee element for scrolling content.&nbsp;</li></ul></ul><h4 style="padding-left: 30px;">Screen Movement Checkers</h4><p style="padding-left: 30px;">Photosensitive Epilepsy Analysis Tool (<a href="https://trace.umd.edu/peat">PEAT</a>) is a software that can also determine the flash rate of an element. However, developers must be careful when using this tool as it may not always provide accurate measurements.</p><h4 style="padding-left: 30px;"><a href="https://www.csun.edu/universal-design-center/compliance-sheriff"><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Compliance Sheriff </span></a></h4><p style="padding-left: 30px;">Compliance Sheriff checkpoint group <strong>Section J</strong> is used to test for screen movement and flickering content in Compliance Sheriff.&nbsp;<span>For more information regarding the checkpoints visit the&nbsp;</span><a href="https://www.csun.edu/universal-design-center/compliance-sheriff-checkpoints">CSU ATI Accessibility Requirements</a><span>&nbsp;page.&nbsp;</span></p><p style="padding-left: 60px;">(NOTE: Compliance Sheriff identifies screen movement but requires a visual test for accessibility compliance)</p>
