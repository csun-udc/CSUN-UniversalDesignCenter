Faculty

Videos, audio, captured lectures, recorded presentations — instructional media — must all have closed captions. This is existing federal law, state law and CSU policy. Regardless of location (classroom, Canvas, etc.), all instructional media with audio must be captioned.

We’re here to help!

Contact NCOD to get captions and transcripts for instructional media.

Steps

  1. Fill out NCOD's New Media Request form at least 6 weeks in advance.
  2. On myCSUNbox, create a folder named “NCOD Captioning – [Professor’s last name]” and give ncod@csun.edu access as Co-Owner.
  3. Upload media and supporting files to this folder.
  4. Within 6 weeks (usually sooner), NCOD uploads captioned media to the “NCOD Captioning – [Professor’s last name]” folder on myCSUNbox.
  5. Post captioned media for student view.
More help:
  • CSUN gives each faculty member their own cloud-based storage account. This service is called myCSUNbox.
  • Contact your department librarian to learn about more offerings from the Oviatt Library.
  • Contact the Faculty Technology Center (FTC) for more assistance with lecture capture.
  • Contact the Universal Design Center with other questions.
  • For Tseng College courses, contact the Distance Learning team.

Live or Real Time Captions

If you will be teaching or presenting live via video, using a videoconferencing tool like Zoom, you’ll want to have someone else handle the captions for you. Learn more about captions in Zoom. Contact us for a list of companies who specialize in live captioning.

Other Videos

First, contact your supervisor or department chair about your department’s budget and procedures for captioning. Then contact NCOD for assistance.

Faculty and staff are recommended to consult with NCOD, however, other options include AutomaticSync Technologies and even do-it-yourself (DIY) captioning.
