Overview 

In accordance with the CSU ATI Procurement Process, when purchasing Information and Communication Technology (ICT) products on campus, all products must demonstrate reasonable accessibility compliance for all students, faculty, staff, and members of the public. As a result, when completing an ICT purchase, all departments must submit documentation regarding a products accessibility prior to purchasing and work with the CSU ATI Procurement Committee and the product vendors to mitigate accessibility issues and concerns in the products being procured. This training is available to all administrative, technical and executive staff and faculty who procure information and communication technology products on campus.

Building an Equally Effective Alternate Access Plan (EEAAP)

Description:  An Equally Effective Alternate Access Plan (EEAAP) is a document that requires departments to record accessibility barriers in a product and establish a procedure to provide equally effective alternate access for the product or service being purchased. In addition, departments purchase Information Communication Technology (ICT) products that have a significant impact on students, faculty, staff and/or public, departments are required to submit an EEAAP to the ATI Procurement Committee.  EEAAP training is a general workshop to assist departments who are required to submit an EEAAP. The EEAAP Training is recommended for faulty and staff procuring ICT products.

Recommended Attendees: Staff, Faculty, Executives, Financial Personnel, Marketing Specialist, Business Analyst, Admin Support, Information Technology Specialist,Web Developers, Web Content Creators, Graphic Designers  

Learning Objectives: 

In this workshop attendees will receive: 

      1. Overview of an EEAAP 
      2. Walk through of EEAAP Form 
      3. EEAAP workshop (Departments are encouraged to bring their EEAAP's for questions) 

Training Schedule: This training will be offered by request only. Go to UDC's Training Request Form to request a training.
