Overview 

Accessibility can be defined by whether or not a document or web application can be used effectively by people with disabilities. Accessibility strives to ensure everyone can perceive, understand, engage, navigate and interact with technology. 

 UDC Training Events: 

At the Universal Design Center we offer the following introductory courses to accessibility:

Universal Design and Accessibility Principles

Description: An introduction of the basic principals of Universal Design and Accessibility. This training is best suited for individuals who would like a general understanding of accessibility and universal design.

Recommended Attendees: Staff, Faculty, Executives, Financial Personnel, Marketing Specialist, Business Analyst, Admin Support, Graphic Designers, and Instructional Support

Learning Objectives: 

In this training, attendees will: 

      1. Receive an overview of accessibility and universal design
      2. Learn about applicable Laws and Standards
      3. Receive a general overview of the CSU Accessible Technology Initiative (ATI)

Training Schedule: This introductory training will be offered two times in the upcoming Fall 2017 semester. To receive a notification on training dates for this event,  complete the Subscribe to Trainings Form.

Introduction to Accessibility and Universal Design for the Web 

Description:  This training will cover information about accessibility laws and standards with an emphasis on the web. This training is best suited for  individuals with knowledge of the web who are new to accessibility and universal design. 

Recommended Attendees: Business Analyst, Information Technology Specialist,Web Developers, Web Content Creators, Graphic Designers

Learning Objectives: 

In this training, attendees will: 

      1. Receive an introduction to accessibility and universal design
      2. Review applicable laws and standards
      3. Learn about how accessibility and universal design apply to technology 
      4. Receive an overview of web accessibility standards 

Training Schedule: This training will be offered by request only. Go to UDC's Training Request Form to request a training.

Introduction to Universal Design for Learning (UDL) and Accessibility  

Description: This training is an introductory course to Universal Design for Learning (UDL) and how it can be applied to course content. By attending this training you will learn first hand what accessibility is, the laws and standards behind it, why its important for your course materials, and what are the CSU and campus policies related to accessibility. This training is best suited for faculty seeking to gain knowledge about Universal Design for Learning and how accessibility plays a role in learning. 

Recommended Attendees: Faculty, Instructional Designers, Instructional Support, Admin Support

Learning Objectives: 

In this training, attendees will: 

      1. Receive an introduction to Universal Design for Learning and accessibility 
      2. Learn about laws and standards 
      3. Review how accessibility and UDL apply to course materials 
      4. Learn about CSU and campus policies on accessibility. 

Training Schedule:  This training will be offered by request only. Go to UDC's Training Request Form to request a training.
