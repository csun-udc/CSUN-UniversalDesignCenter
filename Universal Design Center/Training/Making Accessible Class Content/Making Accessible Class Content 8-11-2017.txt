<h2 id = "beginning" style="color: #ffffff; background-color: #d22030;">&nbsp;What is Accessible Class Content?&nbsp;</h2>
<p>Accessible course content fosters an inclusive learning environment that embraces all learner variability. This approach considers every ability, background, strength, learning style, and much more. Considering learner variability while creating course content, will support the individual needs of every student. These training and guides were developed to support faculty members who would like to incorporate various inclusive learning strategies: <strong><a href="#A1">Creating an Inclusive Syllabus</a>&nbsp;</strong>and&nbsp;<strong><a href="#A2">Publishing Accessible Presentations</a></strong>. The goal is to start including accessibility and universal design principles. The UDC hopes these training and guides will support those next steps.</p>
<h2 style="color: #ffffff; background-color: #d22030;">&nbsp;UDC Trainings and Guides&nbsp;</h2>
<p>The training and guides listed can be reviewed in any sequence and completed using the UDC tutorials or consultant. Please reach out to us if you have any questions <strong><a href="mailto:universaldesigncenter@csun.edu">universaldesigncenter@csun.edu</a></strong>&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<h3 style="padding-left: 30px;"><a id="A1"></a>Creating an Inclusive Syllabus &nbsp;</h3>
<p style="padding-left: 60px;"><span style="color: #cf0a2c;"><em><strong>Description:</strong> </em></span>Writing an inclusive syllabus supports a universal learning environment from the beginning of a course. This training will focus on the basic principles of an inclusive syllabus and general document accessibility considerations. This training is a good starting point for designing accessible course content.</p>
<p style="padding-left: 60px;"><span style="color: #cf0a2c;"><em><strong>Recommended Attendees:</strong></em></span>&nbsp;Faculty</p>
<p style="padding-left: 60px;"><span style="color: #cf0a2c;"><strong><em>Training Objectives</em></strong></span></p>
<p style="padding-left: 60px;">Attendees will:&nbsp;</p>
<ol><ol><ol>
<li>Identify characteristics of an inclusive syllabus&nbsp;</li>
<li>Create and/or redesign syllabus to incorporate all (or some) of the inclusive syllabus characteristics</li>
</ol></ol></ol>
<p style="padding-left: 60px;"><span style="color: #cf0a2c;"><em><strong>Training Format:</strong></em></span> This training is offered by request at the start of each semester. Go to <strong><a href="https://www.csun.edu/universal-design-center/training-request-2">UDC's Training Request Form</a></strong> to request a training.&nbsp;</p>
<h3 style="padding-left: 30px;"><a id="A2"></a>Publishing Accessible Presentations&nbsp;</h3>
<p style="padding-left: 60px;"><span style="color: #cf0a2c;"><em><strong>Description:</strong>&nbsp;</em></span>Creating accessible Power Point presentations will ensure course content is readily accessible to all students. This training will highlight the features already in Power Point to create an accessible presentation. This training is a valuable addition to the designing accessible course content series.</p>
<p style="padding-left: 60px;"><span style="color: #cf0a2c;"><em><strong>Recommended Attendees:</strong></em></span>&nbsp;Faculty&nbsp;</p>
<p style="padding-left: 60px;"><span style="color: #cf0a2c;"><strong><em>Training Objectives</em></strong></span></p>
<p style="padding-left: 60px;">Attendees will:&nbsp;</p>
<ol><ol><ol>
<li>Recognize the features of an accessible presentation</li>
<li>Create and/or redesign a presentation to apply accessible features</li>
</ol></ol></ol>
<p style="padding-left: 60px;"><span style="color: #cf0a2c;"><em><strong>Training Format:</strong></em></span> This training is offered by request only. Go to <strong><a href="https://www.csun.edu/universal-design-center/training-request-2">UDC's Training Request Form</a></strong> to request a training.&nbsp;</p>
