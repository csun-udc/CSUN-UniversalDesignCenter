The first test you can conduct is an accessibility questionnaire. The accessibility questionnaire is comprised a few general questions that are meant to introduce you to accessibility and determine whether or not accessibility is addressed in the product being evaluated. To initiative your accessibility evaluation, spend at least 1 to 2 hours answering the following questions:  

  1. Is the font of text displayed in the software or online product easy to read?

    2. Explanation: If the text displayed on the page is difficult to read, this could be a problem for individuals with various visual impairments.Is the color used in the software or online product difficult to read?

  2. Is the color used in the software or online product difficult to read?

    3. Explanation: If the color displayed is difficult to see, there may be a problem with the color combinations used on the page. The color combinations used on the page may cause individuals with visual impairments difficulties in understanding information on the page. 

  3. Can you use the keyboard “tab” key to navigate to all the button, links, form fields, and features on the page?

    4. Explanation: For this test, you are looking to verify that all functions on the page can be accessed without using the computer mouse. For individuals with physical impairments, it is critical that information can be accessed through other methods instead of the mouse. 

  4. Using the “Ctrl +” key stroke and “Ctrl -“keystroke, can you enlarge the screen without distorting the text?

    5. Explanation: When you make the screen smaller or larger, the text on the screen should continue to be displayed without it being cut off or partially visible.
